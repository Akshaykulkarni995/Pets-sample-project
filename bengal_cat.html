CAT

Bengal Cat

Life Expectancy: 14 to 16 years.

Weight:
Male: 4.5 - 7 kg
Female: 3.5 – 5.5 kg.

Height:
Male: 3 - 3.6 ft
Female: 2.6 - 3 ft

The Bengal cat is known for its soft, sleek coat which has two main fur patterns: spotted (which is most common) and marbled. The most common are brown, snow, silver and blue, though chocolate, charcoal and cinnamon have become more popular in recent times.

It is desirable for Bengals to have white (or light) tummies similar to wild cats. Their eyes are usually green or gold but can also be blue.


Bengal CAT TEMPERAMENT

Bengals do inherit some characteristics of their wild ancestors besides the physical ones. The ALC is an agile climbing cat, and so are Bengals. They enjoy being up high, and owners should provide some climbing trees and perches for the cats to play on. This will save the furniture and drapes! Bengals also do not do well when declawed, as this interferes with their love of climbing and can frustrate them, forcing them to find other outlets for their energy. Declawing can lead to behavior problems in this breed and is not recommended.


INTERESTING BENGAL CAT FACTS
  • The Bengal is revered for its beautiful coat and outgoing personality, but these cats aren't for casual pet owners.
    Bengals are smart and cunning animals that crave attention and are always exploring.
    A Bengal is a handful, but you can rest assured that life with one will never be boring.
